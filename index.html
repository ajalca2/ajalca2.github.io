Introduction

On Ideas, Inquiry,
and the Pursuit of Understanding

A scholar and practitioner engaged with questions of power, institutions, and the human condition — across disciplines and across borders.

Welcome. This site collects my academic work, professional writing, and personal reflections. I am interested in the intersection of political economy, international relations, and the history of ideas — examining how institutions form, why they endure, and when they fail.

My research draws on both quantitative methods and archival sources, with a commitment to writing that is clear and accessible beyond the academy. I believe good scholarship must ultimately speak to the world it studies.

Research

Selected Works

  • 2025

    Sample Paper Title: Institutions and Their Limits

    Journal of Political Economy, vol. 12, no. 3

    This paper examines the conditions under which formal institutions fail to constrain elite behavior, drawing on comparative evidence from the postwar period. We find that institutional persistence is more sensitive to distributional conflict than previous accounts suggest.

  • 2024

    The Architecture of Order: Historical Antecedents of the Modern State

    Working paper · Under review

    Tracing the genealogy of state capacity from early modern Europe to the present, this working paper argues that fiscal extraction and coercive monopoly co-evolved in ways that standard theories of state formation underweight.

  • 2023

    Sovereignty and Its Discontents

    International Studies Quarterly

    A reconsideration of the Westphalian framework through the lens of contemporary norm contestation, with particular attention to humanitarian intervention and the responsibility to protect.

"The purpose of studying history is to watch men grappling with problems — and to learn from their successes and failures."
