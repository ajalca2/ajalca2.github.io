Statement

Research analyst applying to economic Ph.D. programs. Recent projects cover AI effects on labor and productivity, trade policy, and measuring the nonlinearity of the Phillips Curve. These can be found under my profile on the Atlanta Fed's website. Background includes current work with business surveys at the Atlanta Fed's Economic Survey Research Center and previous work on service provider impact evaluations at the University of Notre Dame's Lab for Economic Opportunities. Presently interested in the subfields of contract theory, macro, labor, and political economy. Other interests include political theory, philosophy, theology, and journalism.

Researchers will find my publications and working papers under Research. Essays and commentary appear under Writing. A full curriculum vitae is on the C.V. page.
